Healing in Goodbye

I wonder to myself, “If I have no chance to heal from a disease, what would I do?” This question lingered in my mind, especially when someone I love is facing it. So I asked Akong, “Apa yang akong lakukan kalo gak sembuh?”. I gritted my teeth to fight back the tears as I awaited his answer.

A week later, my sister and I were strolling through the elevator and into my Akong’s room and I blasted through the door like I usually do. But… the first person I witnessed wasn't Akong, it was… Pak Ivan and his gang?!? Yea this wasn't what I expected to see.

“What is happening here?”

Confused, I scanned the room, and realized — “He’s finally gonna get baptized? It's about time!” I muttered. As I went into the room, Akong was lying sideways, clearly to reduce the pain. Just looking at him makes my anus tingle as the sensation of pain strikes through my butt — probably because I'm trying to feel how painful the hemorrhoid feels. He was also being massaged by some random person, probably a person who helps sick people because his massaging technique isn’t wrong. Man, I’m supposed to be his personal massage therapist. I tried to shrug it off. At least Akong is getting some comfort.

He turned his head to face me, smiling as always, as he proudly introduced me to that person. “Oh Ini cucu saya, dia jago mijit!” This is already very normal — most old people like to talk about their grandkids. As much as I wanted to spend time with him, I decided to let him rest for today. So I head home to sleep with thoughts on my mind:

“Yes, he got baptized. But did he want to accept it or was he forced?”
“If he accepts God, does that mean he will be healed?”
“If the leukemia can’t be healed, God definitely still can heal him!”
“I'll come back tomorrow to massage him. Let him rest. He deserves it.”

Except no… instead of entering dreamland, I jolted awake from the sound of sirens.

My heart dropped.

I rushed to the window, looking down while adjusting my eyes and saw... an ambulance?? My heart stopped. No, no, NO! There's no way! Not so soon!

Without thinking, I grabbed my phone to call Akong. Ringing, ringing, ringing… “Nomor yang anda tuju se-” “NAAWWWW!” I screamed as I stabbed the end call with these massage hands. I began to shake as I tried to find words — but they were all mixed up in my head. All I could do was pray as I texted and recalled the past.

“Apa yang akong lakukan kalo gak sembuh?” I had asked him two weeks ago. “Harus sembuh dong,” he had replied — always so optimistic, even though he knew. He always tried to make everything seem fine, even when he obviously was dying inside. I guess I inherited that from him. But now, I am terrified. I didn’t have much time left with him, did I?

RIIINGGG!! My mom’s call snapped me out of the flashback! “Dimana Akong?? Dia tadi kenapa??” I asked as my voice couldn’t maintain a stable tone.

“Cepetan, kasih tahu apa yang kamu mau kasih tahu Akong dulu, dia gak bisa buka HP,” she shouted, worried.

I wrote down the unread message that I sent Akong to my mother. Hopefully, it still wasn’t too late. Seconds felt like minutes, and minutes felt like hours. Finally, his soft and fading reply came through — his final words: “Hehe, Josiah pinter.”

I bit the gum below my lips as tears rolled down my cheeks. Maybe… this is a better end than being healed.

“Goodbye, Akong.”

– Josiah Rephael Dinata
