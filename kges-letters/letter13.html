First Day of Hell

Books—check.
Stationeries—check.
Uniform—check.

I took one last look in the mirror and brushed my freshly cut bangs to prevent them from splitting like my friendships, preparing myself for what I liked to call: the first day of hell.

I’ve always despised school.

But in that dark, torturous place where every shred of hope meets its end, a few lights began to form a sun, melting all the misery with its warm and gentle glow.

Those lights were my friends, my sun, who would always be there for me through both the happy and difficult times.

I was never alone.

But everything changed when my friends moved to a different school. Every. Single. One of them. That same sun would never rise again.

And that awful reality began today.

A new year just started. Once again, I shook hands with the front door and entered the dim room I knew all too well. Dozens of titled chairs were arranged with a bunch of unfamiliar faces sitting on them. The cold, unwelcoming stares greeted me as I sat down and glanced around.

I quickly noticed one guy with camel-like lashes that reminded me of when my best friend joked that we were failures as women. Couldn’t have agreed more. I silently wished for someone who would remotely resemble my friends, but instead, I got a kid who was the spitting image of someone I despised back in 7th grade. And compared to my friends, these people had no taste. One guy literally had broccoli-shaped hair. What could be worse than that?

Same school, same room—different people.

Tik-tok, Tik-tok

Ba-dump, ba-dump

I could almost feel the blood pulse beneath my skin synchronizing with my wristwatch. My throat desperately yearned for that last drop of saliva in my mouth.

I looked to my left. No one. Then right. No one. There’s no one to talk to. None.

I was all alone.

Making new friends felt like my only lifeline. But as soon as I tried to open my mouth, no words came out.

Wait, what am I supposed to say?
This isn’t some sort of school play.
There's no script to follow.
One word could determine everything.
What if they think I’m weird, or creepy??
Argh!! How do you make friends??

?

Wait... How do friendships even start?

I tried to recall all the moments we shared. From when we poured our hearts over the same crush from a TV show. Or the time when we used weird code names for our girl talk. To the sting from accidentally offending my friend, earning my first slap across the face.

But despite everything, we would always forgive one another as our friendship grew stronger.

Our flaws, mistakes, and errors—made our friendship stronger. It wasn’t perfect, so maybe I didn’t need to be perfect today either.

The worries that were swarming my thoughts began to clear up. I clenched my fist, took a deep breath, and regained my composure.

Start over.

I scanned the people who would soon be part of my high school life. Hoping that I could find someone who could be the new sun to rise upon my day. I noticed a girl with long hair beside me, waiting for someone to talk to her. I took the chance and made eye contact. I took a deep breath and prepared myself.

Everything starts with a simple question.

“Hi! What’s your name?”
