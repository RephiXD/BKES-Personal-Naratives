I Love Her

“What if I still like you?” I asked her about it and she was shocked at that moment.

In the morning blue sky, I felt enthusiastic that morning. That day, she and I wanted to go to one of the book fairs. She loves books. I, on the other hand, don’t. I wanted to go that day because she asked me to accompany her to the exhibition.

That morning I picked her up. I was at a loss; words ran through my mind but I couldn't focus or think. I thought to myself “Wow, she is so pretty, her silky hair, with her t-shirt and skirt, her bright face and the scent of her perfume is nice.”

When sentences began slipping out of her, I felt my heart start to pound against my chest. The jittery feeling ran from my arms to the tips of my fingers. If it weren’t for the steering wheel, I wouldn’t know what to do with the overwhelming rush of emotions. I couldn’t even look at her, because if I did, I knew I’d say the wrong things and all of this would be over before it even started. I tried to speak up, even though I was afraid that my conversation would be awkward.

We talked about many things. It was like a dream to be able to talk with her that day. I want to talk to her all the time, even if I don't know what I want to say.

We finally arrived at the book fair. There, I accompanied her to look for interesting books. With a spark of interest myself, I asked her to give me recommendations for books that I should read. After a long time looking for books, she got some that she wanted.

After we finished buying books, we wanted to eat. We bought fries and ice cream then ate in the car. After we finished eating, we headed back to her house.

On the way back, we talked about many things. I spoke to her fluently and she replied with her voice that always made me smile during the trip. Until one moment I asked, “What if I still like you?”

“No way,” She replied, “Do you actually still like me?” She was shocked and silent as she waited for my answer. I nervously answered, “Yes, I still like you”. After that, she asked me why I still liked her until we finally arrived at her house and we parted ways. I don't know what I was thinking at the time.

On one hand, I felt like an idiot. I didn’t know what I was thinking that day. I knew that she didn't like me back, but I still confessed my feelings for the second time. It felt like someone injected me with some sort of strong elixir that sent me a jolt of courage to say that I liked her. It was stupid, really. I knew saying so would make us even more of a stranger than we already are. Not to mention the further awkwardness of talking at school.

On the other hand, I was very happy because I was able to talk to her a lot that day and spend the day with her. Even though she didn't like me back, we still became friends. She changed many things about me and I learned to respect her feelings.

Maybe it was stupid. But regardless of that, it was true. I still did love her and I will continue to love her. Now and, if I had the chance, forever.

– Gamaliel Limbong
