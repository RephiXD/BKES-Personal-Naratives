Quick Snap

A pretty average vacation day, I think. Here I am, sitting in the car, unsure what to do – sleep? Watch old DVDs? Eat snacks? I’ve done everything.

Waiting...

And waiting.. for the front car to move. My only source of entertainment is listening to car horns as my music; it's not like I had a choice anyway. I’m trapped here with my family, who didn’t expect the road to be so full with other “B” license plates.

It isn’t until I open the car door, that the fresh aroma of trees, rain, and soil strikes my nose. They’re somewhat comforting. The sun shines brighter here. All of a sudden, the long car ride is worth it. At this moment, I just wanted to wander around the place, to run, to be free.

“Mom, can we please enter the forest? I want to take a walk there and look for monkeys,” I beg my mom with the biggest puppy eyes to let me in.

"Okay, we can go there, but you have to walk with Oma and Opa okay?” She insists that I walk with my grandparents.

Well, I guess it's better than nothing, so I nod my head with a big grin on my face.

It's not like I hate them, but they’re just… slowing me down.

I reach for my grandparents’ hands, holding them loosely, but they hold mine tightly.
“Don’t walk too fast, Na,” Grandma reminds me once again to walk slowly.
One by one, everyone walked past us, as if we’re being left out.

It's okay, grandparents tend to walk slower.. Right?

I keep walking with them, but their grip starts to feel like handcuffs, trapping me with them.

As if it isn’t embarrassing enough to walk at the very back, they decide to keep on stopping at every single photo spot. I never know why someone would need that many pictures of them in Cimory… I wanted to put a stop to this misery, so when the timing was right, I struck.

“C'mon Na, one more picture and you can go,” Mom says.
“NOOOO, I’m so tired of taking pictures, just let me go already,” my 4th-grade self can’t do this anymore.
“Just one last picture with Opa, and then you can see the monkey over there, it won’t take a long time. Please?” Mom tries to persuade me as if walking with them isn’t embarrassing enough.

Just then my rebellious side took control. My legs start to run, leaving everyone behind sprinting towards the freedom that I’ve been eyeing for a while.

But at what cost?

When I look back, I see it. Their brown eyes squint at me, as their gaze turns weird. Those wordless mouths don’t utter another word. Just a second ago, I thought I had secured my victory. I can feel their disappointed gaze crawling up my skin.

Perhaps, right now, they’re embarrassed to have a granddaughter like me.

It felt like time froze, waiting for me to make a move; should I go back to being trapped, or should I keep running for my freedom?

“Just one more picture with me, please?” Grandpa broke the silence.

“Okay,” I say, trying not to make a scene.

My eyes are set on the camera, trying to maintain a similar expression to smiling, by squinting my eyes, showing my messy teeth, and hiding both of my hands.

One.. two.. three.. smile!

“Now you can go,” finally he lets me go.

It’s a quick snap, but no matter how hard I run, I never outrun the guilt.
