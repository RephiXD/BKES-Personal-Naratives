The Failure of a Lifetime

Sunlight streamed through the gaps of my curtain, as my alarm blared relentlessly, desperately pulling me away from my sleep. I then lifted my half-awake body from the warm, cozy embrace of my bed, reaching for my phone to see my schedule for today.

"Let's see… ooh we have PE today, that should be fun."

I peeked at the other subjects, convincing myself that today might be a good day, until I slightly glanced down, shifting my focus to something else.

"Yikes, there's that one English subject at the end of the day."

All hope suddenly vanished as if the sky had collapsed on me.

English class is indeed unique, much like art class where I get to be creative as long as I produce results. But in reality, the assignments from past years had traumatized me for life.

The first year of senior high school, we had to prepare a TedTalk about, well… anything interesting really. Unfortunately back then I was the quietest and loneliest guy in my class, lacking motivation whatsoever to do homework, moreover sharing knowledge and experience with people I barely knew. Heck, I don't even have any of those so-called experiences because those 2 years in the pandemic I kept indulging myself playing roblox till late in the morning. So of course my presentation went terribly wrong, making me regret not preparing better back then.

The next year came IdeaFest, another presentation type assignment but in groups. And yet I failed again. I couldn't finish editing the video on time which forced us to present a slide that wasn't even done. Again I couldn't prove there is more that I could do.

I couldn't help but wonder, since the last 2 projects had been mentally exhausting, wouldn't grade 12 have the hardest project yet, one that I can't even bear to imagine? Unexpectedly, it's totally different this year…

Personal Narrative

This is perfect! The one thing I can do (or can't possibly mess up to be precise). No need for presentations. All I need is a little passion, creativity, and patience.

There I was thinking for a story to tell, one that would touch people's hearts, that could change people's minds. I'm no writer or bookworm, but people can clearly tell that I had too high of an ambition. The toll took on me, as hours turned into days and days turned into weeks. I skipped all 3 drafts that I was supposed to fill in, eventually leaving one week left before the deadline.

After a prolonged period of pondering what to write, and changing the story multiple times, I finally decided to write about this, my experience these past 2 years in English class. It may have been a hurricane of trouble and way too much failure from my side, but it has been a memorable experience.

The final day when I had to consult my teacher for the first time, I was shaking in fear the whole day, thinking about everything that could go wrong. I kept delaying my progress to the point I thought my teacher must be infuriated.

To my surprise, she was not mad, or at least didn't seem like it. This whole time all I had to do was just do it, "Yes, Nike, there you go," my teacher responded. It had never been about how perfect my story was, but about how I learned in making it. I might have messed up again this time, but I'm proud to say I've moved a few steps forward.

I'm going to miss this last year...

– Vincent Lie
