The Keyboard Warrior

The keyboard warrior. The person who hops online on a device of their choosing, scours the internet, and concocts a 10-page essay as soon as they find an opinion that contradicts their own, pushing the limits of their fingers and keyboard as if their life depended on it.

I was that person once; all the conditions were also met to a T. I also happened to be fighting against another one of my kind, hell-bent on proving his point. The group chat, peaceful and quiet, was suddenly raucous, facilitating a war only fought by two people, with two peacemakers trying to calm the storm.

It wasn't that we were fighting for a noble cause. We weren't talking about world politics, religion, or philosophy. Man, it wasn't even about school. It was the most simple topic: where our civics teacher had moved.

"Ye, pak Calvin Bangun mau pelayanan di Sumatra, jadi mau rayain hari terakhirnya di sini"

Oops. Big mistake. So, as the benevolent friends that my friend and I are, we corrected him. My friend first.

"??? Sumatra? Manado bro"

I chimed in. In a joking manner, of course, as I usually am.

"SuLaWeSi UTaRa"

But then he snapped.

":v"

"yaudah"

"buruk burukin gw"

"gpp"

"gw bodoh kok"

"Bodo amat"

"keep telling me i'm stupid ok?"

That got me pissed. I thought to myself, "This guy is getting on my nerves. We kid around like this when anyone makes a mistake, INCLUDING you, and all of a sudden you get mad? What a joke..." And that's when I decided to put dinner aside, as both of us armed ourselves with our trusty keyboards and went to war.

The opening shot: "WILL YOU SHUT THAT GAPING HOLE IN YOUR FACE, MAN"

Calls for peace were like a whisper in a cacophony of noises. No one was able to put an end to the whirlwind of sentences in the chat. It was like what most wars were like: Loud. Angry. Exhausting. Pointless. What could have been an amicable discussion turned into a furious words-per-minute match.

The war just wouldn't die down. None of us would give in; our keyboards and fingers were at their limits. But then, what seemed to be like water came rushing in.

"It's just I hate whenever he does this, this irritating tantrum of his"

"Me too, but I think fighting a tantrum with another one is a bad move"

It was a slap in the face. I felt so. Dumb. The right thing to do was staring right in my face, but I didn't pay it a single ounce of attention due to my pettiness and pride. "Screw dinner, I wanted to prove myself right for once in my life!" was the only thing going through my head.

It did not last long. Not even a couple of minutes and exhaustion started to kick in; my fingers were starting to get sore. Slowly, but surely, I wanted this pointless drivel to stop; it couldn't go on forever. Besides, I was the one who started this fight in the first place.

It was frustrating. It always does when you have to admit defeat, doesn't it? At least the group chat didn't have two freaks riling up a storm inside it anymore. I had my dinner, relaxed for the rest of the day, and woke up with a clearer mind the next morning. And yet, the damage had already been done. The endless amount of enter-key presses had already been set in stone.

– Maxwell Christian
