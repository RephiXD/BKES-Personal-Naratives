Numbers and Nightfall

Weariness clung to me like an old habit, settling in the moment I stepped into the car.

When the car stopped in front of the house, I opened the door, walked nonchalantly, entered my room, and immediately plummeted to the bed.

Closing the door, only a sliver of golden sunlight slipped through the window, lighting that one spot on the bed.

The deafening silence of the room mirrored the one within. Every thought felt like a problem waiting to be solved, yet none had answers.

Feeling the gravitational pull of the bed, I did not want to move anymore.

The only object that piques my vision is the ceiling, and frequently the math exercise book on the table.

Maybe I forgot to put it back yesterday.

I held the calculus book, intending to put it away.

After lying in bed for too long, I thought going through some math problems would improve my mood, so I opened the book and started to do some math. Numbers were the only thing keeping me sane.

Ugh, why is this so hard? This question on page 9 is so difficult. Why is there a complex number? I thought math was only about real numbers.

Mumbling, and crunching my hair.

I seem to hate math. The graph function of the derivative was making me crazy.

The imaginary numbers of the function?! Aren't imaginary numbers not real?

Fidgeting fingers, red eyes, hatred in my head, everything piled up.

Frustration.

What did I ever do to deserve this?

My state of mind was not in the book anymore.

Leaning down to the table. My vision becomes blurry..

Closing my eyes, I could see her. Smiling, caressing my hair. Comforting me from all of my worries.

Opening my eyes, I could only feel the tears running down my cheek.

She's gone.

I wonder if everything I ever did was real, or if it was just a construct, something that only existed in her mind, never grounded in reality.

My consciousness reverted backward, reminiscing when I did things that felt real, buying her gifts, holding hands while watching a movie, and being there for her as a shelter.

The struggles of the relationship. It all felt real. No complexities of life, just a symplectic life with her.

She was my pearl, delicate and luminous, and I was the box that kept her safe from all the grimes of the world. She was the one who made me feel like I am loved by someone, a meaning for me to live.

But apparently, it all fell apart, pieces that I thought were real, turned into imaginative ones.

Now, the box is empty. I'm still here, just as solid, just as strong, but there's nothing left to protect.

Closing the book, I felt uneasy.

I want to turn back time and fix everything.

The palpation of regret, desire to fix everything. It all made complexities that covered my mind with imaginary things I knew I could not do, even with the tears that were being shed on the white tissue.

At the time I shed the last drop of tears, I opened the book again and continued to the last question I left.

"An imaginary number is not the same as a real number, but side-by-side we could call it a complex number," the book said.

I stared at the definition, feeling the weight of it, but something in me shifted.

Maybe she wasn't an illusion after all—just one part of the complexity of my life, something real, even if I couldn't fully understand it.

– Timothy Sitorus
